The process problem

Software doesn't fix broken process.
Intelligence replaces the need for one.

Most wholesale operations are living inside at least two of the four failure modes right now. Every one of them is a place where Threadline replaces effort with intelligence.

  • 01 / Absent

    No defined way of working.

    Everyone improvises. One rep follows up religiously after every show; another lets orders sit in draft. There's no system because no one ever built one.

    Stitches provides the process. Every rep works from the same living list of what matters today.
  • 02 / Reactive

    Every rule is a scar from a past failure.

    A major order gets lost, so now there's a "check submitted orders every Friday" rule. A brand complains about territory overlap, so now there's a spreadsheet. Always one step behind the last crisis.

    Signals detect the pattern before the break. You catch it in week 2, not month 6.
  • 03 / Overcomplicated

    The process becomes the obstacle.

    A rep needs to fill out twelve fields to log a note from the trade-show floor. So they route around it — text orders, personal spreadsheets. Two realities: the one in the software, and the one running the business.

    Minimal capture. Ten seconds, not ten fields. The system fills in the rest.
  • 04 / Tribal

    The process only lives in your best rep's head.

    The senior rep who's been doing this for twenty years knows exactly when to follow up. It's not written down. When they leave, the process vanishes. The failure mode that kills agencies during succession.

    Workers and Stitches encode what your best reps know. It becomes organizational — not personal.

A note from the founder

Threadline is in closed Beta. We don't have a logo wall yet, and we're not about to invent one.

What we have is a point of view. Wholesale fashion runs on tribal knowledge and spreadsheets held together by the best reps' memory. Every agency I've worked with is living inside at least two of the four failure modes in the philosophy above. No piece of software has fixed that, because software isn't what's broken — the process is.

So we're building the opposite of software. A system that replaces the need for process by surfacing what matters, handling what's repetitive, and remembering what your best reps know. If that resonates, we'd like to build it with you.
